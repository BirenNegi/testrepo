THE SCOPE OF WORKS
3. CONTRACT DOCUMENTS

3.2. (a) examined all the Contract Documents comprising this Subcontract or other information or documentation made available by Vaughan including the Head Contract;

4. VARIATIONS
4.1. The Subcontractor must not vary the Scope of Works under this Subcontract and/or carry out extra work, except as directed by Vaughan or approved in writing by Vaughan.
4.2. The Subcontractor must not commence a Variation until it receives a Variation Order. The Subcontractor must promptly comply with the Variation Order.

5.1. Unless otherwise specified, all materials to be incorporated in Scope of Works must be state of the art.

6. DEFECTS
6.1. If the Subcontractor becomes aware of a Defect it must advise Vaughan as soon as practicable.
6.2. In the event of a Defect, Vaughan may:
(a) direct the Subcontractor to immediately rectify the Defect free of charge by repair or replacement at the Subcontractor’s expense;
(b) accept the work as it is and reduce the Contract Sum by an amount which represents fair compensation to Vaughan, if applicable, for the existence of the Defect; or
(c) after the expiration of the Defects Liability Period or after non compliance by the Subcontractor with a direction under paragraph (a), advise the Subcontractor that it intends to engage others to rectify the Defect.
6.3. If the Defect is not rectified by the Subcontractor within the stated time, Vaughan may rectify the Defect at the Subcontractor’s expense. The reasonable cost incurred by Vaughan in so doing shall be a debt due from the Subcontractor to Vaughan which may be deducted from the Contract Sum or recovered by Vaughan.

The Subcontractor must keep the Scope of Works clean and tidy. The Subcontractor must regularly remove rubbish and surplus materials from the Site. Within 1 day of the Date for Practical Completion of the Scope of Works, the Subcontractor must remove from the Site all constructional plant, surplus materials, rubbish and temporary works of every kind and must leave the Site, existing structures and areas adjacent thereto in as good a state of repair as when the Scope of Works commenced.
TIME
8. COMMENCEMENT, PROGRESS AND COMPLETION
8.1 The Subcontractor must:
(a) commence the Scope of Works by the Commencement Date or such other date as is notified in writing by Vaughan;
(b) proceed with the Scope of Works with due expedition and without delay; and
(c) bring the Scope of Works to Practical Completion by the Date for Practical Completion.
8.2 A failure by Vaughan to provide access to the Site on the Commencement Date shall not be a repudiation or breach of this Subcontract but may entitle the Subcontractor to an extension of time to the Date of Practical Completion.
9. EXTENSION OF TIME
9.1. If the Subcontractor considers that a delay in the progress of the Scope of Works is likely to occur or has occurred, it must give written notice to Vaughan of this. This notice must be given as soon as practicable, and in any event, within 24 Hours after the Subcontractor became aware, or should have become aware, of the event or circumstance.
9.2. The notice must set out a description of the event, the date of commencement of the event, and the nature, cause and likely extent of the delay.
9.3. The Subcontractor shall keep such contemporary records as may be necessary to substantiate any claim, either on site or at another location acceptable to Vaughan.
9.4. Without admitting liability, Vaughan may, after receiving the notice under this Clause 9, monitor the record-keeping and/or instruct the Subcontractor to keep further contemporary records, the Subcontractor shall permit Vaughan to inspect all these records, and shall (if instructed) submit copies to Vaughan.
9.5. Within 5 days after the Subcontractor became aware (or should have become aware) of the event or circumstance giving rise to the claim, the Subcontractor shall send Vaughan a fully detailed claim which includes full supporting particulars of the contractual basis of the claim.
9.6. If:
(a) progress of the Scope of Works is delayed such that the Scope of Works might not reach the stage of Practical Completion by the Date for Practical Completion;
(b) the delay is caused by a Delaying Event; and
(c) the Subcontractor has otherwise strictly complied with this clause;
then the Subcontractor shall be entitled to an extension of time of the Date for Practical Completion equal to the time of the delay.
9.7. Where more than one event causes concurrent delays and at least one of those events, but not all of them, is not a Delaying Event, then, to the extent that the delays are concurrent, the Subcontractor shall not be entitled to an extension of time for Practical Completion.
9.8. The right of the Subcontractor to make a claim for an extension of time pursuant to this Clause is the Subcontractor’s sole remedy in respect of any delay or delays, disruption, prolongation or loss of productivity. The Subcontractor is not entitled to any increase or adjustment to the Contract Sum or any other monetary compensation or damages (including damages for breach of contract or negligence) as a result of any such delay.

DEFAULT & DISPUTES
20. DEFAULT & DISPUTE RESOLUTION
20.1 If the Subcontractor commits a Performance Default Vaughan may give a notice in writing requiring the Subcontractor to show cause why Vaughan should not proceed in accordance with its rights upon a Termination Default. The Subcontractor must, within the time specified in the notice (or if no time is specified within 5 Business Days) give good reason why Vaughan should not proceed in accordance with its rights upon a Termination Default. 20.2 If the Subcontractor commits a Termination Default Vaughan may suspend payment under this Subcontract and may, by notice in writing, take the whole or any part of the Scope of Works remaining to be completed out of the hands of the Subcontractor, exclude the Subcontractor from the Site and complete the Scope of Works by any other means. Should the amount so certified be greater than the further amount which would have been paid to the Subcontractor if the whole of the Scope of Works had been completed by the Subcontractor, the difference between the two amounts shall be a debt due from the Subcontractor to Vaughan.
20.3 Both parties to this Subcontract shall endeavour to settle any differences or Dispute arising in connection with this Subcontract in an amicable manner and in accordance with the conditions of this Subcontract.
20.4 If a Dispute between Vaughan and Subcontractor arises in connection with this Subcontract or the subject matter hereof, then either party shall deliver to the other party a notice of dispute in writing adequately identifying and providing details of the Dispute.
20.5 Notwithstanding the existence of a Dispute, Vaughan and the Subcontractor shall continue to perform the Subcontract and, subject to a Termination Default by the Subcontractor, the Subcontractor shall continue with the Scope of Works under this Subcontract.
20.6 Within 7 days of service of a notice of dispute, the parties shall confer at least once to attempt to resolve the Dispute or to agree on methods of resolving the Dispute by other means. At any such conference each party shall be represented by a person having authority to agree to a resolution of the Dispute.
20.7 If the Dispute has not been resolved within 14 days of service of the notice of dispute, that Dispute shall be and is hereby referred to arbitration.
20.8 Arbitration shall be effected by an arbitrator nominated by the Chairperson for the time being of the Victorian Chapter of the Institute of Arbitrators Australia.
20.9 Unless the parties agree in writing, any person agreed upon by the parties to resolve the Dispute pursuant to Clause 20.6 shall not be appointed as an arbitrator, nor may that person be called as a witness by either party in any proceedings, other than for evidence that Clause 20.6 steps were satisfied.
20.10 The award made by the arbitrator shall be final and binding on both Vaughan and Subcontractor and neither party shall be entitled to commence or maintain any action upon any such Dispute until such matters have been referred and determined by the arbitrator.
